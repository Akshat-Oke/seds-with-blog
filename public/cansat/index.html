Rocket CanSat CubeSat R&D
Rocket CanSat CubeSat R&D

Team JANUS

A CanSat is a miniature satellite payload.

The CanSat container will have a compact Avionics Bay consisting of Pressure, Rotation & GPS sensors to collect accurate data. Apart from the modeling, designing, software development, sensor integration, and manufacturing, the CanSat will be tested on several bases, including recovery, release mechanism, construction quality, and material performance. The tests are Drop, Thermal, Vibration, and Vacuum.

Force Analysis

Considering parameters like drag force, decent velocity, the density of air, etc., the areas of the two parachutes are calculated. These factors are also used to determine the appropriate material for fabricating the container body.

Container

The design of the container depends on multiple factors. The type of payload to be designed is the primary determining factor of container design. It is modeled to meet the requirements of the mission.

Parachute Bay

Activated audio beacon and the latest obtained GPS data will be used to locate the container and payload. The parachute's fluorescent orange & pink color will make it easily detectable.

Battery

The Electrical Power subsystem ensures that the power sources are sufficient to power the container and payload for 2 hours.

Avionics Bay

GCS

  • The Ground Control Station consists of a laptop, Xbee radios, and a hand-held antenna. It is used to receive and transmit data to the CanSat.
  • The interface is designed with Python 3.9. The ground station will command the CanSat to start transmitting telemetry data before the launch.
  • The Xbee will receive data from the container, and GCS software will read and store the data and perform real-time plotting.

Payload

CDHS

  • The command and data handling subsystem processes and handles the data.
  • The Teensy microcontroller processes and samples data from the sensors.
  • This data is primarily stored in the SD card.
  • It is also sent to the secondary storage device, the EEPROM, in case of processor data reset.
  • The Xbee module facilitates data intercommunication between the container, payload and GCS.

FSW

The Flight Software reads data from the sensors, the ground station, and stores, analyzes and processes the data for flight control and on board deployments. The onboard deployments include:

  • Controlling the release of the parachutes and payload at appropriate heights.
  • Maintaining the orientation of the payload camera.
  • Managing GPS and Audio Beacon function for the successful location after landing.
